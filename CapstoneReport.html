Information Entropy as Measure of Quality of Reviews.

Introduction

We can state that a review of a business is of good quality if it is well written and contains useful information for prospective costumers. I consider that discerning between good and bad reviews at the moment they are submitted is an important issue for sites like Yelp, users of such sites and business alike. Quality reviews should be given more prominence at the moment of evaluating a business or presenting information to the users of review sites.

The main objective of this work is to propose a non-parametric measure of quality of reviews submitted by Yelp users. Under the assumption that Yelp's elite members write, on average, reviews of good quality I will say that the measure is effective if the standard deviation of a significant sample of users that had elite status at least during one year is significantly lower than that of the general population. We will say that a measure of quality is effective if the standard deviation of the quality values of population tagged as elite is lower than the standard deviation of the general population with a significance level of \(p \leq 0.05\). The main hypothesis is that at least one of the proposed measure will meet this criteria.

The measures proposed are based on Shannon's Entropy, which is a measure of the information content of a string in terms of the distribution of its elements. A complete introduction of the theory behind the functions proposed and their properties is beyond the scope of this work and the stated 5 pages limit.

Methods and Data

In information theory, Shannon's Entropy of a string \(S\) is defined as: \[H(S) = -\sum_{i}P(c_i) \text{log}_2 (P(c_i)),\] where \(c_i\) is each of the different characters (with respect a dictionary) of the string \(S\) and \(P(c_i)\) is the distribution of each character among the string \(S\), Formally \(P(c_i) = \#(c_i)/|S|\), where \(\#(c_i)\) is the number of times that \(c_i\) appears in \(S\) and \(|S|\) is the longitude of the string.

The Proposed Functions.

For this project I analyze six variations of Shannon's Entropy as a measure of quality. These variations have the form: \[H_d(X) = -\sum_{i}P(x_i) \text{log}_2 (P_D(x_i)),\] where the object \(x\) can be a character, like in the original definition, or a whole word and \(P_D\) is a probability distribution function in of the following three categories: local, universal and relative.

  • Local distributions are based on the string \(X\), separated in either characters (letter L.H. for short) or words (word L.H.).

  • Universal distributions are based on the concatenation of all strings in a sample set, separated again in words (words U.H.) or characters (letter U.H) .

  • Relative/Conditional distributions are based on the concatenation of all reviews in a sample set on which the user held elite member status at least once (letter C.H and word C.H respectively).

The Data

The data collected consist on 15,000 randomly chosen reviewers (without replacement) and the entropy value of all their written reviews seen as a single string. After gathering the text, all the six functions are applied to the concatenated review text of each user. In the case of word entropy, the punctuation signs, parenthesis and line escapes ('!','?', '.', '(', ')' and 'New line') are removed before separating words by spaces. For character entropy all characters are considered, including special characters. In total, 8,479,520 words were considered of which 233,505 were unique and 32,675,544 characters were collected of which 333 were unique.

Statistical Model for Evaluation

As stated in the introduction, under the assumption of the existence of an optimal quality value and that elite members write better reviews more often than the general population, for any measure of quality, the set of values for a quality function should show lower variation among the population of elite members than that of the general population. The converse should also hold.

Given above's argument, to determine which, if any, of the proposed functions represent a good measure for the quality of the text I propose to use the standard deviation on the set of the outputs on each of the six functions. In other words, if \(\sigma_{P}\) is the standard deviation of the general population and \(\sigma_{E}\) is the standard deviation of elite members, then \(\sigma_{P}-\sigma_{E} > 0\) and the bigger this difference is, the more accurate is the measure. In order to determine if this difference is significant we will use the P-value, fixing the required significance level at \(p \geq 0.5\).

Now, gathering the data for the initial sample took several days of computing time hence sampling a significant number of similar sets is not feasible. For this reason, bootstrapping is used to estimate the \(p\) value by resampling with replacement 100 sets of 1000 randomly chosen pairs from the original sample set in order to obtain an estimate of the distribution of the differences of the standard deviations.

Discussion

For this project I proposed and tested six related, but different functions of information entropy as a measure of quality of the review text submitted by Yelp users.

Under the assumption that members that held elite status at least once submitted reviews of better quality at a higher rate than the general population, any quality measure should show lower variation among the elite population, hence this standard deviation differential was used to test the proposed measures. The function chosen is based on the Shannon's entropy of the words used in a review. This function yielded the biggest standard deviation differential and was also shown to be statistical significant.

Given the presented results, word based Shannon's entropy is a good measure of quality that can be used to weight the reviews submitted without the need of other input.
